Matt Clark - Personal Website

SPITFIRE DTP Oceanography PhD Student, University of Southampton, National Oceanography Centre Southampton, UK.


I am currently investigating North Atlantic forcing of the European shelf seas and slope current over a multi-decadal timescale. I use a variety of assimilated observational datasets, model output and lagrangian particle tracking simulations to achieve my aims. As well as my research, I enjoy demonstrating for several physical oceanography undergraduate modules.

Outside of academia, I am an experienced hiker. I have organised and led day trips of up to 100 students around Hampshire and the Isle of Wight. I have also led small groups on more challenging weekend-long trips to some of the more remote areas of the UK. During the weekends and my days off, you can usually find me deep in the countryside with my camera.

About this site

The aim of this site is to provide a hub of all my work and research-related web profiles, to better showcase myself. The blog will also share my day-to-day experiences of doing a PhD.

I have built this website myself. It is a "static" HTML5 website with Bootstrap, based on the free open source templates and guides by Start Bootstrap. Thanks to Bootstrap's use of Flexbox, the website displays well on almost any screen size. It's cookie, tracking/analytics and advertising free, so fully GDPR compliant. The site is currently freely hosted by GitHub Pages.
