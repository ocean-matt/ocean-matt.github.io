My PhD Research

Project title: Changing Atlantic influences on northwest European shelf seas

My current research focuses on the changing North Atlantic Ocean and its effects within European shelf seas. Using model outputs and observational datasets, I will analyse how the shelf seas are driven by basin-scale change over different timescales. There will be a strong emphasis on quantifying changes in temperature and salinity structure in the North Sea. I am excited to be taking an interdisciplinary approach to this: I am interested in how both physical and chemical changes to the water structure alter shelf sea ecosystems and productivity. I will also be using measurements of plankton biomass from the Continuous Plankton Recorder (CPR) survey to assess any possible relationships.

My PhD research follows on from my MSci final year research project, which quantified a cold temperature anomaly in the North Atlantic Subpolar Gyre and its effects on transport towards and along the European shelf edge.

I am supervised by Prof. Bob Marsh (University of Southampton), Dr. James Harle (National Oceanography Centre) and Prof. Martin Edwards (Marine Biological Association/CPR Survey).

Teaching & Demonstrating

As well as doing my research, I am a postgraduate demonstrator (essentially a teaching assistant) for several undergraduate modules in the School of Ocean and Earth Science at the University:

  • SOES1004: Physics Of The Ocean (formerly Physical Oceanography 1). I demonstrate some boatwork techniques on board RV Callisa. As this is a first year module, this is often students' first time on a research vessel.
  • SOES1013: Key Skills for Marine Scientists. I have led a Meteorology fieldcourse component, which focusses on marine meteorological observations and forecasts.
  • SOES2010: The Dynamic Ocean (formerly Physical Oceanography 2). Assisting with boatwork & Matlab tutorials.
  • SOES3010: Large Scale Ocean Processes & Climate. Matlab tutorials.
  • SOES6014: Introduction to Physical Oceanography. I assist with a basic calculation tutorial for new MSc students.
  • SOES6018: MSc Key Skills and Literature Review. I show the Masters students how to use the CTD and ADCP systems on board RV Callista.

In light of the COVID-19 pandemic, teaching and demonstrating has, for the most part, moved to online platforms for the 2020-21 academic year. I have however still been demonstrating for some COVID-secure boatwork sessions on RV Callisa.

Sea-going research experience: RAPID cruise, March 2020

My PhD research does not include any fieldwork, and what fieldwork I have done previously as part of my MSci was on board the University vessel RV Callista for only a few hours at a time. So in March 2020, I volunteered as a core science team member on the RAPID Cruise to get valuable insight and practical experience of observational oceanography at sea. My scientific roles included:

  • Taking 2-hourly meteorolgical observations.
  • Taking 4-hourly seawater samples for salinometer analysis.
  • Assisting with the deployment and recovery of oceanographic moorings (see video below).
  • Daily processing of bathymetry, navigation and underway sampling data, using some Matlab scripts and programs.

For a detaled summary of life at sea on the RAPID cruise was like, please read my Challenger Wave article "The Last Ship" and blog post "Moorings: The workhorse of the RAPID project" which summarises the key components of the moorings we were servicing.
